# Brainstorm Summary: The Path to the Math Void

*A record of the philosophical evolution of Project "New World"*

## Step 1: The Rejection of Teleology
We began by questioning why creatures in simulations are forced to "walk." We realized that explicitly rewarding distance is a "Designer's Bias." True life doesn't have a goal other than survival. We decided to strip away explicit goals and rely purely on implicit survival metrics (Energy).

## Step 2: The Rejection of Biology (The Spark)
We discussed Abiogenesis. We realized that survival isn't a "desire" but a mathematical filter. We debated "The Spark" (programming a copy command) vs letting chemistry naturally form self-replicating loops.

## Step 3: The Rejection of Earth Physics
We questioned why we should use gravity, water, or vibration. If we give a block a "Sensor" tag, we are playing God. We decided we must only program raw Thermodynamics (Heat/Kinetic force) and let the creatures *invent* sensors through physical reactions like thermal expansion.

## Step 4: The Discovery of Mechanical Mitosis & Arbitrary Space
We realized that we do not need a `Copy()` command at all. Reproduction can emerge purely as a mechanical failure (tension snapping a structure in half). We also realized Space is just a variable, making Teleportation a valid, fundamental law of physics.

## Step 5: The Limit of Human Imagination (The Noumenon)
We hit a philosophical wall: human brains cannot imagine truly new physics because we evolved in this universe. To escape this bias, we must abandon Earth physics entirely.

## Step 6: The Ultimate Conclusion (Evolving the Laws of Physics)
Instead of hardcoding rules, we decided to build a "Multiverse Generator." The universe is just a 2D array of floats. The computer randomly generates mathematical equations (combining Sin, Mod, +, etc.) to act as the Physics Law for that universe. 
We let the computer test thousands of random mathematical laws until it finds one where stable, self-replicating patterns emerge from the static. We are not evolving life; we are evolving the universe itself to find one that breeds life.
